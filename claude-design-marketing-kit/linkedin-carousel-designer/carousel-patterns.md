# LinkedIn Carousel Patterns

Reference material for `linkedin-carousel-designer`. Specs, hook formulas, slide density rules, ending patterns, and post-copy conventions.

---

## LinkedIn Carousel Specs

- **Format:** PDF upload (LinkedIn calls these "document posts")
- **Recommended dimensions:** 1080 x 1350 (vertical, 4:5) — best engagement
- **Acceptable alternatives:** 1080 x 1080 (square), 1080 x 1920 (story-style)
- **Slide count sweet spot:** 10 slides. Range: 8-12. Beyond 12, engagement drops.
- **File size:** <100MB
- **No animations, no interactive elements** — static only

## 14 Hook Slide Formulas

Every high-performing carousel starts with a hook slide that stops scroll. These are the 14 patterns that consistently work on B2B LinkedIn.

### 1. Controversial claim
"Most sales leaders are measuring the wrong thing."

### 2. Counterintuitive stat
"73% of pipeline is fake. Here's how to find the 27% that isn't."

### 3. "I was wrong about X"
"I spent 8 years doing X. I was completely wrong."

### 4. "Stop doing X"
"Stop running pipeline reviews. Here's what to do instead."

### 5. Problem most people ignore
"Nobody in RevOps wants to talk about this."

### 6. "Here's what [role] doesn't tell you"
"Your CFO doesn't trust your forecast. Here's why."

### 7. Transformation story
"From 40% forecast accuracy to 94%. Here's how we did it."

### 8. Framework reveal
"The 5-step diagnostic every RevOps team should run quarterly."

### 9. "If you're X, read this"
"If you're a VP Sales at a B2B company 200-1000 people: read this."

### 10. Public mistake admission
"I almost killed our pipeline last quarter. Here's what I learned."

### 11. "The truth about X"
"The truth about why reps fudge their forecast."

### 12. "X lessons from Y years doing Z"
"12 lessons from 10 years running B2B SaaS revenue."

### 13. Prediction
"By 2027, every B2B sales forecast will be AI-verified. Here's why."

### 14. Definition-based reframe
"Most people define 'pipeline' wrong. Here's the definition that changes everything."

## Information Density per Slide

**The rule:** One idea per slide. Maximum 35 words of body copy.

If a slide is getting cramped:
- Split it into two slides
- Cut half the words
- Replace text with a visual element (icon, chart, arrow)

**Why:** LinkedIn carousels are consumed on mobile, often in 2-3 seconds per slide. Dense slides get skipped.

**Measurement:**
- Headline: ≤10 words
- Body: ≤35 words
- Total slide text: ≤45 words

## Ending Patterns

### Pattern A: CTA Ending
Final slide drives to a specific next step.

**Example:**
- Headline: "Want the full diagnostic?"
- Body: "Free download — 15-point pipeline reality check."
- CTA: Link in comments / URL on slide

**When to use:** You have a concrete offer to drive to. Don't fake a CTA if you don't have one.

### Pattern B: Summary Recap Ending
Final slide summarizes the main points in a scannable list.

**Example:**
- Headline: "The 5-point recap"
- Body: numbered list of the points made earlier

**When to use:** Educational carousels. Makes the carousel save-worthy.

### Pattern C: Save-Worthy Checklist
Final slide is a practical checklist users will want to save for later.

**Example:**
- Headline: "Your pipeline diagnostic checklist"
- Body: 5-7 checkbox items

**When to use:** Tactical, how-to carousels.

### Pattern D: Provocative Question
Final slide asks a question that invites comment engagement.

**Example:**
- Headline: "What's your answer?"
- Body: "Comment below — are you running this diagnostic or not?"

**When to use:** Thought leadership carousels where you want debate.

## Accompanying Post Copy Conventions

The post that accompanies a carousel does 3 jobs:

1. **Stop scroll** (first line must be the hook)
2. **Set expectation** (what the carousel delivers)
3. **Invite interaction** (swipe, save, comment, share)

### Structure (4 parts)

```
<Hook — first line, matches carousel slide 1 verbatim or very close>

<Expansion — 1-2 sentences on why this matters right now>

<Preview — what the carousel walks through>

<CTA — "Swipe through" or "Save this" or "Share with your revops team">
```

### Hook match rule

The first line of the post should be the same line (or very close) as the carousel's Slide 1 headline. This creates a consistency signal that rewards the swipe — users feel they're getting exactly what was promised.

### Character count

- LinkedIn truncates after ~150 characters. Front-load the hook.
- Total post length: 100-250 words performs best.
- Don't write essays — the carousel is the content.

### Hashtags

- 3-5 is optimal. More than 5 looks spammy.
- Use topical tags (#RevOps, #SalesOps, #B2BMarketing) not generic (#marketing, #business).
- Place at the bottom of the post — LinkedIn doesn't reward front-loaded hashtags.

## Carousel Anti-Patterns

- **Too many slides with the same structure.** Mix headline-heavy, icon-led, data-viz, quote-focused slides.
- **All text, no visual.** At least 3 slides should have a visual element as the primary content.
- **Burying the hook.** Slide 1 must hit the thesis. Don't "warm up" with context slides.
- **Cramped text.** If you're ≤10pt at full res, you're too dense.
- **Inconsistent brand.** Same color palette, same type system, same icon style across all slides.
- **Weak CTA slide.** If the final slide isn't driving action, replace it with a strong summary.
