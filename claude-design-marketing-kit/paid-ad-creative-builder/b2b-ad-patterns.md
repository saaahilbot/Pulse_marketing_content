# B2B Ad Patterns

Reference material for `paid-ad-creative-builder`. Six proven B2B ad formats, copy-to-visual ratios, platform specs, and creative testing framework.

---

## 6 Proven B2B Ad Formats

Each format has a specific job and performs best in specific contexts. Match format to audience state.

### 1. Social Proof ad

**Pattern:** "Join <number> of <target role>"

**When to use:** Mid-funnel, audience is aware but hesitant. Needs de-risking.

**Example copy:**
- Headline: "Join 5,000+ revenue leaders who forecast by reality, not hope."
- Body: "See how [Your brand] reads your CRM + call data to surface the deals that'll actually close."
- Visual: Grayscale customer logo bar as the hero visual, brand color background, CTA button in accent.

### 2. Customer Story ad

**Pattern:** "How <Customer> achieved <specific outcome>"

**When to use:** Mid-to-bottom funnel. Audience is evaluating and wants proof.

**Example copy:**
- Headline: "How Acme closed 40% more deals in one quarter."
- Body: "Their VP Sales swapped pipeline theatre for real forecasting. Here's the 20-min walkthrough."
- Visual: Customer logo + outcome metric in large type, brand subtle in background.

### 3. Comparison ad

**Pattern:** Direct contrast — "X vs. Y" or "Tired of <competitor pain>?"

**When to use:** Bottom funnel, competitive takeaway campaigns.

**Example copy:**
- Headline: "Tired of activity-tracking dashboards that don't predict anything?"
- Body: "[Your brand] is the only platform that scores every deal on close probability, continuously."
- Visual: Split screen — "Before" (chaotic) vs. "After" (clean), brand colors apply to the "after" half.

### 4. Stat Shock ad

**Pattern:** Big number + implication

**When to use:** Top funnel, awareness. Stops scroll with a claim that feels uncomfortable.

**Example copy:**
- Headline: "73% of your pipeline is fake."
- Body: "Here's the 15-point diagnostic revenue leaders use to find what's real. Free."
- Visual: Massive stat in hero type, minimal decoration, brand accent on one element only.

### 5. Problem / Solution ad

**Pattern:** "Your <audience> is <problem>. <Brand> <solution>."

**When to use:** Any funnel stage. Workhorse format. Best when you can be very specific about the problem.

**Example copy:**
- Headline: "Your reps are drowning in CRM data."
- Body: "[Your brand] surfaces the signals that predict close — automatically. Book a demo."
- Visual: Iconography or simple illustration of the transformation (before → after).

### 6. Founder POV ad

**Pattern:** "I <experience>. Here's what I learned."

**When to use:** Top funnel or brand awareness. Authentic-feeling, high engagement when the founder is credible.

**Example copy:**
- Headline: "I ran RevOps for 10 years. Here's what every forecast gets wrong."
- Body: "The short answer: it's built on rep intuition, not deal signal. Here's the fix."
- Visual: Founder photo (professional, not corporate headshot), pull-quote framing, brand subtle.

## Copy-to-Visual Ratios

Platform-specific guidance.

### LinkedIn Sponsored Content

- **Headline on creative:** ≤70 characters. Longer gets truncated in feed.
- **Body copy on creative:** ≤150 characters.
- **Text-to-image ratio:** ≤20% of the creative surface is text. More than that looks like a slide.
- **Feed-level text (the post itself):** 100-200 characters works best. Longer versions can work but front-load the hook.
- **CTA button:** "Learn more", "Download", "Register", "Sign up" — keep it to platform defaults.

### Meta Feed (Instagram + Facebook)

- **Headline on creative:** ≤40 characters. Mobile-first — smaller viewport.
- **Body copy on creative:** ≤125 characters.
- **Text-to-image ratio:** Meta historically enforced a 20% rule; now officially relaxed, but performance still drops when text dominates. Keep text-heavy sections compact.
- **Feed-level text:** 80-125 characters ideal. Users expect less reading on Meta than LinkedIn.
- **CTA button:** Platform defaults only.

## Platform Specs (as of 2026)

### LinkedIn Sponsored Content (single image)

- **Dimensions:** 1200 x 627 pixels
- **File types:** JPG, PNG
- **File size:** 5KB minimum, 5MB maximum
- **Aspect ratio:** 1.91:1
- **Safe zones:** Avoid placing critical copy in the bottom 20% (feed mobile can crop)

### LinkedIn Sponsored Content (square)

- **Dimensions:** 1200 x 1200 pixels (newer format, mobile-optimized)
- **File types:** JPG, PNG
- **File size:** Same as above

### Meta Feed (single image)

- **Dimensions:** 1080 x 1080 (square) or 1080 x 1350 (portrait, higher engagement)
- **File types:** JPG, PNG
- **File size:** 30MB maximum
- **Text-to-image:** Use Meta's text overlay preview tool before launch

### Meta Stories / Reels-adjacent

- **Dimensions:** 1080 x 1920 (9:16 vertical)
- **Safe zones:** Top and bottom 250px reserved for UI — keep critical content centered vertically

## Creative Testing Framework

### Variant volume

- **Minimum viable:** 6 creatives per campaign (3 per platform if running both)
- **Ideal:** 9-12 creatives per campaign (3-4 angles × 3 visual treatments)
- **Why:** LinkedIn and Meta algorithms need variant volume to optimize. A single creative starves the algorithm.

### Rotation cadence

- **First 48 hours:** Let algorithm find the winner. Don't pause variants prematurely.
- **Days 3-14:** Keep winners, pause losers (CTR <0.5% on LinkedIn, <1% on Meta).
- **Day 14+:** Watch for creative decay — CTR usually falls 20-40% between week 2 and week 4 on the same creative.

### Creative decay signals

- CTR has dropped ≥25% from peak
- Frequency has climbed above 3-4 impressions per user
- Negative feedback has ticked up ("hide this ad" signals)

When decay hits, refresh the creative (new visuals, new hook angles) — don't just swap a color.

---

## Creative Testing Pitfalls

- **Testing too many variables at once.** Test one variable (headline, visual, CTA) at a time if you can.
- **Testing without enough volume.** Under 10K impressions, you're reading noise, not signal.
- **Assuming best LinkedIn creative works on Meta.** Different aspect, different audience mindset — test separately.
- **Not separating by audience segment.** A creative that wins against VP Sales may lose against CFOs. Segment audience in the platform's reporting.
