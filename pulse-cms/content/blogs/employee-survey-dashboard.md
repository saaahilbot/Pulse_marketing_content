# Employee Survey Dashboard: What to Track and Why

A good employee survey dashboard doesn't show you everything — it shows you what requires action right now.

The difference between a dashboard that informs decisions and one that creates noise is the hierarchy of information. Here's what every HR dashboard should surface, and in what order.

## The Core Four Metrics

### 1. Engagement Index
A single composite score (typically 0–100) that aggregates all survey responses into one number. This is your headline metric — it should be visible, trend-able, and benchmarked against prior quarters.

**In Vantage Pulse:** Displayed as a circular gauge with an up/down indicator vs. prior period. Current demo environment shows 65 (↑4 vs prior).

### 2. eNPS (Employee Net Promoter Score)
"On a scale of 0–10, how likely are you to recommend this company as a place to work?"
- Promoters (9–10): actively advocate for the company
- Passives (7–8): satisfied but not enthusiastic
- Detractors (0–6): actively disengaged, attrition risk

**Benchmark:** Industry average eNPS is 10–30. AccessOne achieved eNPS 45 with Vantage Pulse.

### 3. Participation Rate
The percentage of employees who completed the survey. Below 30% means your data is statistically unreliable. Above 60% means you're getting a genuine organisational signal.

**In Vantage Pulse:** Displayed with a participation alert badge — "Low Participation" when below threshold, so HR knows to investigate before over-interpreting scores.

### 4. Category Scores
Scores across your engagement dimensions — Alignment, Autonomy, Compensation, Wellness, Recognition, etc. Each category is colour-coded:
- Green (≥70%): Strong
- Yellow (60–69%): Neutral
- Red (<60%): Needs Attention

## The Heatmap: Where the Real Insight Lives

The overall scores tell you *what*. The heatmap tells you *where*.

A heatmap cross-tabulates engagement categories against departments (or geographies, demographics). It's how you find that your Wellness score of 68 is actually driven by a single department — R&D — sitting at 41, while every other team is above 75.

**In Vantage Pulse:** The heatmap covers 2,100+ responses across 14 departments with red→green colour coding. A score of 04 in Technical Support (Engagement row) is immediately visible.

## The Comments Feed: Qualitative Context

Numbers tell you the what and where. Comments tell you the why. The Comments & Sentiment Analysis page surfaces:
- Positive/Neutral/Negative sentiment per comment
- AI-generated Overall Feedback Summary with key themes
- Word cloud per open-ended question
