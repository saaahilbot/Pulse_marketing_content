# Employee Engagement Survey Analysis: A Step-by-Step Guide

Collecting survey data is the easy part. Analysing it — finding the signal in the noise, identifying the highest-priority issues, and translating scores into specific actions — is where most organisations struggle.

This guide walks through the analysis process from raw data to decision.

## Step 1: Check Participation Validity

Before analysing anything, verify your participation rate. If a department has fewer than 5 responses, individual responses may be identifiable — treat that data with care and consider combining it with adjacent teams for reporting.

General guidance:
- Org-wide survey: 40%+ participation for statistical reliability
- Department-level analysis: minimum 5–10 responses per segment

## Step 2: Identify Your Outliers

Start with the extremes, not the averages.

**Lowest scores:** Which categories score below 60? Which departments score below the company average on every metric? These are your urgent priorities.

**Highest scores:** What's working? Identifying strengths is as important as finding weaknesses — you need to understand what to protect.

**Biggest movements:** What changed most vs. the prior period? A score dropping 15 points in one quarter needs immediate attention regardless of its absolute value.

## Step 3: Cross-Segment the Data

An organisation-wide engagement score of 72 can mask a department at 44. Always segment:

- By department
- By geography (if multi-site)
- By tenure (new hires vs. veterans often have very different scores)
- By manager (if your org has department managers with enough reports for anonymity)

The heatmap is your most powerful analysis tool. Red cells at the intersection of a department and category point directly at the problem.

## Step 4: Read the Comments

Quantitative scores tell you *what*. Open-ended comments tell you *why*. Before jumping to solutions:

1. Read comments from your lowest-scoring departments
2. Group similar themes manually, or use AI-generated summary to extract key themes
3. Look for themes that appear in comments but don't show up in your scoring categories

AI-assisted analysis (like the Overview Feedback Summary in Vantage Pulse) can process 1,827+ responses in seconds, surfacing: "Compensation review requests increasing" or "Remote work flexibility is a key satisfaction driver."

## Step 5: Prioritise Actions

Not every red score requires the same response. Prioritise by:

- **Severity:** Score below 50 is critical; 50–60 is concerning
- **Reach:** How many employees are affected?
- **Trend:** Is it declining fast, or has it been stable?
- **Strategic importance:** Is this team critical to a key company goal?

## Step 6: Build the Action Plan

For each priority issue, define:
- What specifically will change
- Who owns the action
- The 30/60/90 day milestone
- How you'll resurvey to verify improvement
