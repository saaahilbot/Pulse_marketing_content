# Catchy Employee Engagement Survey Names

The name of your employee survey matters more than you might think. A generic "Q3 Engagement Survey" gets opened reluctantly. A name that signals purpose, trust, and value gets opened with curiosity.

## Why Survey Names Matter

A well-named survey:
- Signals that leadership takes feedback seriously
- Sets expectations about scope and time commitment
- Builds psychological safety before the first question

## Names by Tone

### Professional & Clear
- Voice of the Team
- People Pulse
- Culture Check
- The Listening Session
- Our People Report
- Speak Up Survey
- The Feedback Window
- Team Pulse Check

### Conversational & Approachable
- How Are We Really Doing?
- What's On Your Mind?
- Let's Talk
- Your Turn to Lead
- The Honest Hour
- Tell It Like It Is

### Forward-Looking & Positive
- Building Tomorrow Together
- Your Voice, Our Direction
- Shape Our Culture
- The Better Workplace Survey
- Your Blueprint
- Future Forward Feedback

### Brief & Punchy
- Quick Pulse
- The 5-Minute Check
- Rapid Feedback
- Fast Pulse
- Micro-Listen
- The Quick Read

### Themed for Specific Topics
- The Wellness Check-In (mental health surveys)
- Return Ready (back-to-office surveys)
- First Impressions (onboarding surveys)
- The Long View (tenure anniversary surveys)
- One Year Strong (1-year anniversary)
- The Kickoff Check (new manager surveys)

## Tips for Naming Your Survey
1. Keep it under 5 words
2. Make it sound human, not corporate
3. Hint at the time commitment ("Quick" or "5-Minute")
4. Avoid anything that sounds evaluative or high-stakes
