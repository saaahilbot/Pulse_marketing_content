# Employee Survey Tools: What HR Leaders Need to Know

The employee survey software market has exploded. There are dozens of platforms — from lightweight form builders to enterprise engagement suites. Choosing the wrong one doesn't just waste money. It wastes your employees' time, generates data you can't act on, and erodes the trust you were trying to build.

## The Three Categories of Survey Tools

### 1. Form Builders (Google Forms, SurveyMonkey, Typeform)
General-purpose tools adapted for surveys. Low cost, easy to set up, but:
- No automated analysis or insights
- No HRIS integration
- No anonymity guarantees
- Results require manual processing
- No action planning workflow

Suitable for one-off surveys in very small organisations. Not suitable for ongoing employee listening programmes.

### 2. Engagement Platforms with Survey Features
Tools like Culture Amp, Lattice, or 15Five include pulse surveys as part of a broader performance management suite. They offer more depth than form builders, but:
- Survey capabilities are often secondary to their core use case
- Can be expensive to access the full feature set
- May require separate licences for different HR functions

### 3. Dedicated Pulse Survey Platforms
Purpose-built for employee listening — surveys, analytics, insights, and action planning in one place. Vantage Pulse is in this category.

**What dedicated platforms offer:**
- Automated survey scheduling and reminders
- Real-time dashboards with engagement index, eNPS, participation rate
- Heatmaps across departments and demographics
- AI-generated summaries and response suggestions
- Lifecycle automation (onboarding, tenure milestones)
- Frontline access via kiosk and QR code
- Compliance certifications (GDPR, SOC 2, ISO 27001)

## The Hidden Cost of Cheap Tools

A form builder might seem free. But consider:
- Hours of manual data processing per survey cycle
- No automated reminders = lower participation = less reliable data
- No benchmarking = no way to know if scores are good or bad
- No action planning = insights that never get implemented

The real cost of a cheap survey tool is the HR time spent on work the platform should do automatically.
