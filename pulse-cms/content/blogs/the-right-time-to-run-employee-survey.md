# The Right Time to Run an Employee Survey

Timing is the most underrated variable in employee survey design. The best-designed survey with the clearest questions will fail if it lands during the wrong moment.

## Times to Avoid

**Performance review season.** Employees are already anxious. Survey scores will skew negative regardless of actual engagement levels, and they'll associate the survey with evaluation pressure.

**Immediately after layoffs or restructuring.** You'll get raw, reactive responses that don't reflect ongoing sentiment. Wait 4–6 weeks for the initial emotional wave to settle.

**Major product crunch or delivery sprint.** Timing a survey when your engineering team is shipping a product launch guarantees low participation and high frustration.

**End of financial year.** Finance and sales teams are heads-down. Participation will be low from exactly the groups whose sentiment often matters most.

## High-Value Timing Windows

**Onboarding milestones.** Days 7, 15, 30, 45, 60, and 90 are natural checkpoints. New employees have fresh perspectives and high motivation to share them. Automated lifecycle surveys hit these windows without any manual scheduling.

**Post-major-changes.** After a new manager joins, a return-to-office policy launches, or a significant benefit change is rolled out — these are the moments when employee sentiment shifts fastest.

**Tenure anniversaries.** A 1-year, 3-year, or 5-year employee has context and history that a new hire doesn't. Anniversary-triggered surveys capture institutional knowledge and long-term sentiment.

**Mid-quarter, mid-week.** The least disruptive survey timing for most organisations is Tuesday–Thursday, roughly six weeks after quarter start. Employees are in productive routines rather than sprint mode.

## The Case for Automation

The real answer to survey timing isn't a perfect calendar — it's removing the calendar dependency entirely. Lifecycle automation triggers surveys based on employee events (joining date, anniversary, manager change) rather than a fixed schedule, which means every survey arrives at the moment it's most relevant.
