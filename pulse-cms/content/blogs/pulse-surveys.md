# Pulse Surveys: The Complete Guide for HR Leaders

A pulse survey is a short, frequent employee survey — typically 2–15 questions — sent on a regular cadence (weekly, fortnightly, or monthly) to track employee sentiment in near-real-time.

The name comes from the medical analogy: just as a pulse check gives immediate information about health status, an employee pulse survey gives immediate information about organisational health.

## Why Pulse Surveys Are Replacing Annual Surveys

Annual surveys were the standard for decades. They still have a role, but they have a fundamental flaw: they measure how employees felt on the day they filled it in, averaged across a company that has changed dramatically since the survey was designed.

By the time results are analysed, presented to leadership, and actioned — typically 3–6 months later — the problems they surfaced may have resolved, escalated, or been eclipsed by new ones.

**The case for pulse:**
- Real-time signal vs. quarterly or annual snapshot
- Higher completion rates due to shorter format
- More frequent data allows trend detection
- Lower survey fatigue when questions are varied
- Faster action cycle — results → insights → response in days, not months

## How Pulse Surveys Work

**1. Frequency:** Monthly is the standard cadence for most organisations. Weekly is appropriate for teams going through major change. Quarterly is too infrequent to catch fast-moving issues.

**2. Length:** 3–8 questions per cycle. Mix rating scale (1–5) questions with one open-ended question. Rotate question sets to avoid respondents auto-filling answers.

**3. Automation:** Best-in-class platforms like Vantage Pulse automate delivery, reminders, and data aggregation — removing manual overhead from HR entirely.

**4. Anonymity:** True anonymity (platform-level, not just policy-level) is non-negotiable. Without it, participation drops and responses sanitise toward the safe middle.

## Pulse vs. Annual Survey: When to Use Each

| Factor | Pulse Survey | Annual Survey |
|---|---|---|
| Speed of insight | Days | Months |
| Response length | 3–15 questions | 40–80 questions |
| Frequency | Monthly/quarterly | Once or twice a year |
| Best for | Ongoing temperature check, trend tracking | Deep organisational benchmarking |
| Action speed | Immediate | Slow |
| Employee effort | Low | High |

## The 3-Phase Pulse Process

**Phase 1 — Survey:** Quick, mobile-friendly, anonymous check-in. 3–8 questions. Takes 2–5 minutes.

**Phase 2 — Insights:** Automated dashboard with Engagement Index, eNPS, category scores, and heatmap. HR sees results in real time without manual analysis.

**Phase 3 — Action:** AI-generated summaries surface the top priorities. Action Planning module tracks what HR commits to change and by when.

## Interpreting Pulse Results: What the Scores Mean

- **Score ≥70:** Strong — this dimension is a cultural asset
- **Score 60–69:** Neutral — monitor, watch for trends
- **Score <60:** Needs Attention — prioritise for action
- **Score <40:** Critical — immediate intervention required

The AccessOne case study illustrates this well: their Customer Success department showed a Relationship with Peers score of 20 (critical). Without the heatmap, this would have been invisible against an organisation-wide average that looked acceptable.
