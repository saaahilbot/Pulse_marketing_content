# Confidential vs. Anonymous Surveys: What's the Difference?

These two terms are often used interchangeably, but they mean very different things — and the distinction directly affects how honest your employees will be.

## Confidential Surveys

In a **confidential survey**, the organisation collects identifying information (name, email, department) but promises not to share it publicly. HR or the survey platform *can* trace a response back to an individual if needed.

**The problem:** Employees know traceability exists. Even if it's never used, the awareness suppresses honest answers — especially on sensitive topics like management effectiveness or compensation fairness.

## Anonymous Surveys

In a **truly anonymous survey**, no identifying information is collected. The platform is designed so that even the survey administrator cannot link a response to a specific individual.

**The result:** Higher participation rates and more candid responses. IBM research shows 83% of employees say they would participate in an employee listening program — but only if anonymity is guaranteed.

## Why It Matters for Your Data Quality

A confidential survey about management effectiveness will underreport problems. An anonymous survey on the same topic will surface them. The difference in data quality isn't small — it's the difference between knowing you have a problem in the Customer Success team and not knowing.
